What ECOA is
Core Definition & Foundational Position

ECOA (Empirical Concept Observation Architecture) is the observational realization architecture of DALE. Its role is to transform admissible abstract system observations into structured empirical observation states while preserving observational admissibility, structural coherence, traceability, readable reconstruction, and architectural continuity.


ⓘECOA operationalizes observation. It does not redefine DALE, replace DALE, or function independently from DALE foundational architecture. ECOA exists downstream from DALE foundational logic and upstream from DALE tool execution.
!ECOA is not a tool. Tools are downstream observational containers. ECOA is the architecture within which tools operate. Tool convenience must never redefine observational structure.

      Structural status logic
      Six Canonical Structural Statuses

      ECOA does not treat missingness as neutral. Silence ≠ absence. Absence ≠ inactivity. Non-observability ≠ missing data. These distinctions must remain explicit throughout realization and must not collapse into neutral values or silent omissions.

      ✗Converting missingness to neutral values, silent omission, UI-based status collapse, default substitution, hidden inference, and reporting simplification that removes statuses are all structurally invalid. They introduce false coherence or break traceability.

      Adaptation governance
      Adaptation Boundary
      Governing Adaptation Law
      "Observable realization may adapt. Foundational observational architecture may not."

      Adaptation exists because different systems require different observational visibility. Healthcare systems, governance systems, enterprise systems, localization systems, climate systems, education systems, and community systems may require different observable variables, operational structures, and family organizations. However, contextual adaptation must not redefine foundational architecture.

      !Clients, implementers, and tool builders do not modify DALE foundational architecture. They may adapt within canon-governed constraints only if traceability, admissibility, observational continuity, reconstruction dependence, structural status recoverability, and foundational DALE laws remain intact.
      Canonization process
      Canonical Governance Flow

      Adaptation becomes official only after canonization. Uncanonized realizations remain experimental — not official ECOA realizations.
