What CBC is
Purpose & Canonical Role

CBC is the governance reconciliation and adaptive value exchange layer of the DALE architecture. It organizes traceable structural outputs into governance coordination architectures, legitimacy structures, negotiated value exchange systems, accountability pathways, trust verification systems, participation architectures, adaptive stewardship structures, and cross-scale governance continuity systems.


CBC does not begin with contracts. It begins with structured system signal already processed through CoS, ECOA, ARA, Runner, and SEC (where applicable). Every governance structure it produces must be traceable to upstream system conditions.


ⓘ"CBC does not invent legitimacy. It surfaces how legitimacy operates, where governance disconnects emerge, and what remains unresolved across actors and scales."
Key insight
Cross-Scale Governance Recognition
CBC recognizes that actors operating within the same system often govern through different realities. CBC functions as the reconciliation infrastructure between these realities — not as a replacement for either.
Macro Realities
Metrics · Compliance · Reporting · Operational controls · Financial accountability · Institutional governance
Micro Realities
Relational legitimacy · Trust · Lived participation · Social memory · Informal coordination · Adaptive community governance
!Governance breakdown often occurs when upstream abstractions lose coherence with downstream legitimacy structures. CBC's purpose is to preserve governance continuity across all three scales.
Involves vs. does not involve
CBC Scope
CBC Involves
  • Organizing traceable governance structures
  • Mapping obligations, legitimacy, benefits, participation, and trust flows
  • Reconciling upstream metrics with downstream governance realities
  • Structuring adaptive value exchange architecture
  • Exposing governance asymmetries and coordination tensions
  • Preparing systems for accountable coordination and stewardship
  • Preserving unresolved governance conditions explicitly
CBC Does NOT Involve
  • Imposing governance detached from system reality
  • Producing symbolic participation structures
  • Treating compliance as legitimacy
  • Replacing CoS, ECOA, ARA, Runner, or SEC
  • Hiding instability behind reporting frameworks
  • Treating negotiated agreement as structural resolution

Activation logic
CBC Activation Conditions

CBC is NOT automatically activated. Runner determines the realization pathway. No practitioner decision overrides the realization type determination from Runner.

RT-SEC Enterprise first — SEC activates, CBC may follow
RT-CBC Governance reconciliation — CBC activates directly
RT-H Hybrid — SEC first, then CBC receives CTP
RT-U Unresolved — do not proceed
Conditions
When CBC Activates
CBC activates (RT-CBC or RT-H) when:
  • Value realization requires formalization of negotiated value exchange
  • Participation continuity requires governance coordination
  • Governance coordination requires formal reconciliation structures
  • Institutional legitimacy requires traceable accountability
  • Benefit accountability requires formal governance structures
  • Stewardship alignment requires negotiated obligation systems
  • Trust verification requires observable governance continuity
  • Adaptive obligation structures require formal coordination
Direct Runner → CBC (no enterprise creation needed) when:
  • Negotiated governance without enterprise creation
  • Benefit formalization without enterprise creation
  • Participation agreements without enterprise creation
  • Adaptive accountability without enterprise creation
  • Institutional reconciliation without enterprise creation
  • Resource stewardship without enterprise creation
  • Contractual coordination without enterprise creation
!In RT-H, CBC Phase 1 (GIP) absorbs the CBC Transition Package (CTP) from SEC Phase 7 as a primary upstream input. The SEC CTP is the formal handoff between SEC and CBC.
Process structure
Seven-Phase Process Overview

The CBC process moves through seven sequential phases. No phase produces free-form governance drafts — every output must be traceable to upstream structural inputs. No phase may be skipped.

✗The Governance Accessibility Package (GAP) requires completion of Phases 1–6. Both institutional and community-facing outputs are required in Phase 7 — producing only one side reproduces the governance asymmetry CBC exists to address.

Governance coherence
Nine Governance Coherence States

CBC introduces four governance-specific states beyond the SEC baseline. RES-LR, RES-AR, RES-TR, RES-F, and RES-U are valid expected outputs — do not force RES-C.

✗CBC does not fabricate legitimacy. Governance tensions and asymmetries are structural findings — they must be preserved, not eliminated. Treating compliance as legitimacy is explicitly not permitted.
Coherence dimensions
Five Coherence Dimensions (Phase 4)
DimensionKey QuestionFailure Condition
Legitimacy coherenceDoes participation align with governance reality?Participation is symbolic — not connected to real decision-making
Accountability coherenceAre obligations structurally enforceable?Obligations exist in documents but not in operational structures
Trust coherenceCan verification remain operationally credible?Reporting mechanisms are disconnected from lived outcomes
Authority coherenceCan governance function beyond concentrated authority risk?Governance depends on single actors or informal relationships
Flow coherenceDo governance flows reinforce each other?Value, obligation, trust, and authority flows fracture under pressure
Phase 7 requirement
Comprehension Asymmetry

CBC recognizes that governance asymmetry often emerges through comprehension asymmetry — institutional outputs may be legible at macro scale but illegible at the last mile, and vice versa. Phase 7 must produce both.

Institutional Outputs (Macro)
Upstream Operational Requirements
Compliance frameworks, reporting structures, financial accountability, institutional governance formats. Legible to funders, regulators, institutional partners.
Community-Facing Outputs (Micro)
Downstream Interpretability
Governance summaries accessible to community actors. Relational legitimacy formats. Participation-enabling representations. Social memory preservation.
✗Producing only institutional outputs in Phase 7 reproduces the structural governance asymmetry CBC exists to address. Both output types are required.

Terminal Position
CBC as Final DALE Layer
CBC is the terminal governance layer of the DALE architecture. Unlike CoS, SEC, and intermediate tools, CBC outputs feed directly to external stakeholders — institutional partners and community governance — as well as the master dataset.
Cross-Scale Traceability
GovernanceScale Field
Every CBC output record carries a GovernanceScale field (macro / meso / micro / cross-scale). This makes the scale of governance coherence explicit and traceable — not assumed equal across all actors and levels.
