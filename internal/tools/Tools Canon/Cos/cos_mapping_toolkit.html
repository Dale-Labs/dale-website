What this tool does
Purpose & Scope

The CoS Mapping Tool is the Diagnostic/Sensor layer of the DALE architecture. Its function is to capture system signal from a submitted observation package — a finite family of admissible abstract inputs — and transform them into structured empirical observations through the ECOA pipeline.


It does not evaluate, rank, score, or prescribe. It observes. Every output it produces is a structured empirical state, not a judgment.

ⓘ
One execution = one submitted package of abstract inputs under one concrete observation condition. The tool does not determine which inputs to submit or how to reformulate later packages.
Inputs
What enters the tool
  • Submitted package of candidate abstract inputs
  • Concrete observation condition (sector, geography, engagement type, mode)
  • Practitioner ID and timestamp
Outputs
What the tool returns
  • Fundamental empirical state (v1–v40)
  • Operational empirical state (X1–X12)
  • DALE family states (D/A/L/E)
  • Canonical output records with full traceability
  • Noise log for non-admissible inputs
  • Balance-relevant missingness log

Fixed global frame
Five Global Principles
These five principles are the fixed structural backbone of the architecture. They are not guidelines or themes — they are formal structural laws that govern admissibility, decomposition, signed-state determination, and empirical assignment. They cannot be redefined during observational execution.
⚠
No operational variable, local system, metric transformation, or empirical output may introduce principles external to this fixed set. Every variable must be expressible through at least one of these principles and at least one of their associated model families.
P1 Participant-grounded legitimacy ▶
Domain
Identity, dignity, agency, disclosure, participation, readiness, safety, context-shape
Structural law
Admissible formation must begin from identity-bearing, dignity-bearing, agency-preserving, safe, and non-coerced participant conditions. Formations that bypass participant legitimacy, safety, agency, or admissible disclosure are architecturally inadmissible.
Model family (M1)
Tree of Life · Identity Mapping · Context–Pattern–Support · Facilitator Role · Archetype Mirror · UTAUT Facilitation Lens
Subsumes
Identity preceding enterprise · psychological safety as structural precondition · no forced vulnerability · participant control over disclosure · dignity restoration · experience preceding naming
P2 Structural observation before intervention ▶
Domain
Observation, system description, problem formation, causal depth, design direction
Structural law
Admissible action, change, or movement must be preceded by structural observation, system describability, and problem clarification. Systems that bypass observation do not support admissible bounded movement.
Model family (M2)
Community Operating System · System Blueprint · Cause Ladder / 5 Whys · Fishbone / Cause–Effect · Boundary Tagging · Gap–to–HMW
Subsumes
Observation before action · system description before change · root-cause distinction · clarity before ideation · design beginning with the right question
P3 Explicit relational differentiation for decision-relevant system readability ▶
Domain
Actor position, influence, gatekeeping, value structure, burden distribution, risk distribution
Structural law
Relational asymmetries, actor-position differences, structural flows, and decision-relevant empirical signals must be made explicit and non-collapsed.
Model family (M3)
Influence Map · Gatekeeper · Value Flow · Burden–Benefit–Risk · Four-Flow Overlay · Audience Triangle · Audience Segmentation · Impact Map · Impact Management Lens
Subsumes
Distinguishing power from title · making invisible influence explicit · separating beneficiary/user/customer/payer · separating value/burden/benefit/risk
P4 Context-fit and bounded movement ▶
Domain
Movement, intervention fit, boundary logic, partnership readiness, procurement access
Structural law
Admissible movement must fit context, position, timing, safety, readiness, and explicit boundaries. Fit has priority over force.
Model family (M4)
Move Fit · Partnership Readiness · Procurement Cycle · Negotiation / BATNA
P5 Adaptive continuity with local agency ▶
Domain
Continuity, leadership, revisability under learning, localization, community agency, coordination
Structural law
Admissible systems must preserve continuity, revisability under learning, local agency, and operational coordination discipline.
Model family (M5)
Leadership Shift · Continuity Pathways · DALE Four-Element Agile Impact · Agile Impact Implementation · Community-Driven Data · Localization Value-Chain · Standards-of-Practice · Integrated Accountability System · Implementation Roadmap

12 Operational Variables (X1–X12)

Observational pipeline
Six-Step Transformation Logic
The CoS Mapping Tool executes a deterministic observational pipeline. For fixed architecture, fixed admissible input, and fixed observation condition, exactly one internal path is admitted. No alternative path is generated during execution.
A_adm → S_in → R_term → Δ (signed states) → E_fund → E_op → E_a (abstract return)
1
Common Input-System Observation
The submitted admissible package is placed inside one common DALE-grounded frame before individual recursive realization proceeds. Abstract inputs are not treated as an isolated list — they are co-observed as one input-system configuration.
Operator: S_in(A_adm, c) = {P_proj, M_proj, A_adm, Ξ(A_adm, c)}
2
Admissibility Check
Each candidate input is checked against 7 admissibility conditions. Non-admissible inputs are classified as form_noise, decomposition_noise, or architectural_noise. They are recorded in the noise log but do not enter the observational process.
Predicate: χ_in(a) ∈ {0, 1}
3
Recursive Decomposition
Each admissible abstract input is decomposed through its operational realization, then through lower-level realized subsystems under the same fixed architecture, until every branch reaches a terminal realized subsystem satisfying the stop condition.
Operator: Φ(a, c) = R_term(a, c) — uniquely determined, no alternative path admitted
I
Type I: Fundamental closure — V(S) ⊆ V_fund. Assign local signed states, then empirical values.
II
Type II: Non-observability — V(S) ∩ V_proj = ∅. Record status: no_observable_variables.
4
Local Signed-State Realization
At terminal Type I subsystems, each fundamental variable receives a local signed observational state in [−1, 1] under the governing principles and models of that subsystem. Explicit informational absence is recorded when no admissible data exists.
δ_R(f; a, c) ∈ [−1, 1] ∪ {∅_info} — these are NOT yet final empirical outputs
5
Metric Assignment at Fundamental Layer
Final empirical assignment aggregates local signed states across all terminal subsystems containing the same fundamental variable. If no non-absent signed states exist, explicit informational absence is recorded — never a default or neutral substitute.
μ_Φ(f; a, c) = mean(D_f(a, c))  ·  κ(x) = 2·(x−m_min)/(m_max−m_min)−1 for metric transformation
6
Upward Reconstruction
Operational empirical states are reconstructed from the fundamental layer using fixed admissible bases. DALE family states are then reconstructed from contributing operational states. Finally, abstract-level empirical return is formed for each admissible input.
F_X → E_op → G_S → E_{D/A/L/E} → F_a → E_a(c)
⊗
Not permitted during execution: redefining principles or models, introducing a third variable class, inserting neutral/default values for informational absence, admitting an alternative decomposition path for the same architecture-input-condition triple, or determining how future packages should be reformulated.
Output logic
Status Logic & Structural Distinctions
The architecture distinguishes six structural conditions that must never be collapsed into one another.
assigned — valid empirical value in [−1, 1]
info_absence — activated, data missing
struct_absence — admissible, not activated
balance_relevant_missingness — required by governing principles, absent from terminal realization
⊗
info_absence is never replaced by a neutral value (0), a default value, or an inferred completion. If required admissible information is absent, the status is recorded explicitly. This is a structural condition of the observational result, not a gap to be filled.

Structural Outputs (generated by pipeline)
Layer 1
Fundamental Empirical State
Common empirical substrate. Basis for all higher reconstruction. Activated v1–v40 with values or info_absence.

Abstract-Level Empirical Return
Empirically reconstructed state of each admissible abstract input. Arithmetic mean of assigned operational values.
